This visualization tries to give both a high and low level insight into the environmental impact of product lifecycles by offering a "helicopter view" over the involved processes and products (i.e. fields).
The underlying data is of a hierarchical nature, meaning that every process is the aggregate of its subprocesses. As such, all fields can be subdivided into smaller fields, where the size of a field indicates the impact of the corresponding process/product relative to the whole landscape in view.
The menu, or "roadmap", above every landscape indicates the subject of the landscape that is in view (circle 1). Circle 2 indicates how many layers lay on top of the current landscape, while circle 3 indicates how many underlying layers are displayed in the current landscape. This menu can be used to navigate through the landscape by clicking on the circles.

The color of a field indicates its impact normalized over all fields of all landscapes. In other words, a nice green landscape is preferred over an arid brown landscape. Water (i.e. blue fields), are not part of the landscape and indicate the improvement in relation to the worst landscape.
